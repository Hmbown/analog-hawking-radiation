Overview
--------

The Analog Hawking Radiation Simulation Framework provides a computational framework for simulating analog Hawking radiation in laser-plasma systems, with a focus on robust horizon detection, realistic multi-beam configurations, and practical detection feasibility assessment.

Key Features
~~~~~~~~~~~~

* **Robust Horizon Detection**: Advanced algorithms for identifying analog event horizons in plasma flows with uncertainty quantification
* **Multi-Beam Superposition**: Realistic modeling of multi-beam laser configurations with power-conserving field superposition
* **Quantum Field Theory Integration**: Direct calculation of Hawking spectra from first principles
* **Detection Modeling**: Radio-band radiometer modeling and integration time calculations
* **Bayesian Optimization**: Parameter space exploration with probabilistic horizon modeling

Scientific Motivation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Analog Hawking radiation explores quantum field effects in curved spacetime through laboratory analogs. This framework specifically investigates laser-plasma systems where high-intensity laser interactions create effective horizons that mimic gravitational effects.

The theoretical foundation includes:

* **Hawking Radiation Theory**: Quantum particle creation near horizons
* **Analog Gravity**: Fluid/optical systems mimicking gravitational effects

Research Objectives
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

This research addresses the following core questions:

1. How can we robustly identify horizon formation in laser-plasma systems?
